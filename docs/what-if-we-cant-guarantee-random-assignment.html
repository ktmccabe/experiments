2.5 What if we can’t guarantee random assignment?

Example: Selection into treatment

What if we didn’t have the independence? Subtract and add \(E[Y_i (0) | D_i=1]\).

\(E[Y_i (1) | D_i=1]-E[Y_i (0) | D_i=0] =\)

\(\underbrace{E[Y_i (1) | D_i = 1] - E[Y_i (0) | D_i=1]}_{\text{Average treatment effect for the treated}} + \underbrace{E[Y_i (0)|D_i=1]-E[Y_i (0)| D_i=0] }_{\text{Selection bias}}\)

In observational studies, where assignment into treatment is not random, the second term may not be zero. - E.g., suppose we want to know the effect of min. wage laws on unemployment. - Laws aren’t randomly assigned - Possible that states where unemployment (outcome) is lower are less likely to have min. wage laws passed relative to states where unemployment is higher
